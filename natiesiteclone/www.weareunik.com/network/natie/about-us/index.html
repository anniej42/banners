WE ARE NATIE

Advertising agencies are kind of funny. They love to talk about innovation…but they’re usually not very innovative.

Natie is different. We do more than talk about new ways of communicating and working; we work that way ourselves.

We have a small core team in Tel Aviv, but truly, we’re a global agency in the best sense of the word. Our multi-talented international team has been telling brand stories in every medium for more years than we care to remember.
We work together virtually because we all share the same values, the same aesthetic and marketing sensibilities and most important, the same commitment to making great work for our clients. Just about the only thing we don’t share is physical proximity. Because today, we don’t have to.

We specialize in branding, animation, storytelling and design. We’re interactive, we write, and we create for mobile, digital, social media and broadcast. We make collateral, brochures and direct mail. And we do some stuff you’ve probably never even heard of, too. Our network of global thinkers and doers includes professionals from every marketing discipline.

And working this way works. Our track record proves it. We have some great clients, we collaborate and partner with other agencies and we consult, too. When we say full-service, we mean it. (Meaning what we say is one of our rules.) We work with or have worked with Google across four continents, Apple, YouTube, DHL, Yohji Yamamoto, Better Place, Ogilvy and Mather, BBDO, Volkswagen, Target, AT&T, American Express, Toyota, Waterlogic, Southern Comfort, Groopti, Citibank, Rhodium, Arnold Worldwide, Alexandra Mor, Leonard Cohen’s Fund for Reconciliation, Tolerance and Peace, TBWA and more.

Eitan Chitayat, our founder and fearless leader, has spread the Natie gospel at conferences in equally far-flung centers of global business. Being passionate about what we do, working hard at it and holding ourselves to the highest creative standards have gotten us a seat at some very impressive tables. When clients want a team that’s nimble and collaborative – a team whose work is as much a pleasure to see as their people are a pleasure to work with – increasingly, they call us.

But however we work and whomever we work with, we always have the same goals: to create the best work possible, to deliver results and to make you, the client, very happy. (As well as us.)

Eitan
Founder / Creative Director
Israel
Work hard and be nice.

Eitan Chitayat is a global citizen. He’s also the founder of our agency.
A creative director, copywriter and brand-builder, Eitan’s been delivering marketing campaigns for B2C and B2B clients for over fifteen years. He has lived in Hong-Kong, London, Tel Aviv, New York and Boston, and he was recently a judge for one of advertising’s finest global competitions, The One Show.
Leading Natie and while surrounded by dedicated teams both near and far, he’s made remote creative work the agency’s specialty. He thrives on making stuff and forging solid relationships, all while delivering smart creative work he credits to Natie’s amazing global team.

Eitan has overseen and played a part in award-winning work on integrated campaigns across all mediums, most notably for Google, Apple, Facebook, YouTube, TJX, Volkswagen, Target, DHL, American Express, Yohji Yamamoto, Toyota and AT&T. He’s worked at Arnold Worldwide in Boston, Ogilvy & Mather and BBDO in New York and was TBWA Digital’s executive CD in Tel Aviv. He also consults and speaks at conferences – meet him and you’ll quickly notice he’s not shy!
“Work hard and be nice” are the words he lives by.

Nicola
Designer
Italy
Design is one.

Nicola was born in Italy, he lives in Italy and we envy him for being there. But though he was born in a small town near Naples in the south of the country, his spirit is quite worldly.

He’s studied classical literature in Latin, Italian and ancient Greek, but he always had a knack for design. In fact, he was a designer in the fashion trade, but after ten years of that, he began to study graphic and web design. He promptly fell in love with Massimo Vignelli’s work (thanks, Eitan!) and the modernist movement. This passion paved the way for him to earn a spot at one of Naple’s top ad agencies, where he worked on national campaigns, websites and countless brand identity projects.

Nicola is a typography freak and wrestles daily (and in his sleep) to find harmony between colored pixels and white space. He expresses creativity in any medium, is a published design veteran and has won a few international prizes along the way. A seasoned teacher of web and graphic design, he is an instructor at one of Italy’s most renowned communications institutes. And if you could draw DNA from one of his cells, ‘creativity’ would be written on them in Helvetica.

His dream is to build a better world where language, whether designed or written, is beautiful. He has adopted his mentor’s belief that “design is one, not many different ones.”

Some advertising folks are left brain; some are more right brain. Dan can only be described as whole brain.

Insatiably curious, keenly interested in consumer behavior and a lover of the original and unexpected, Dan is a strategist’s strategist.

His sharp, nimble approach is the hard-won result of a twenty-year journey that began at Procter & Gamble and Sara Lee, where he developed breakthrough strategies for several well-known brands. Dan hails from Manchester in the UK, so his Man United fandom is totally legit. His strategy for enjoying life is to play footy with his kids and to explore Israel from the seat of his bike, Lycra shorts and all.

Irene is like our resident Lisbeth Salander, and that’s not fiction.

Born and raised on a small island in the gulf of Naples, she loved putting puzzles together as a child. Drawing and creating and refining her skills, she progressed all the way to the beautiful murals she painted on her living room walls at age five. Irene attended art school in Naples, where she studied architecture and interior design. Later, she went on to study graphic design and web design at one of Italy’s top communications institutes and graduated with honors. To top off her education, Irene also studied php programming and followed that up with a job as a developer at a communications agency.

Don’t ask us why, but Irene loves all things Scandinavian – the cold, the music (Norwegian black metal) and literature in the form of Norse mythology. When she isn’t programming, you might find her reading comics or playing video games. And dreams? Yeah, she’s got a few. One of them is traveling to view the northern lights at their highest point.
